## How to Read This Document

Each NPC profile contains:

- **Public role** — what everyone on the floor knows
- **Private goal** — what they actually want; never stated to the player
- **Contradiction** — the gap between their presentation and their reality; the game lives in this gap
- **Voice pattern** — how they speak: cadence, vocabulary, tells
- **Arc beats** — the major story moments by year; question numbers are approximate anchors, not exact content
- **Relationship thresholds** — what unlocks at each score band; effects are cumulative
- **Career trajectory** — where they go independent of the player's choices; the world moves whether or not the player is watching

**On the relationship score:** All NPCs start at a pre-modifier base score before first contact. The Reputation modifier applies at introduction: +10 per 20 points of Reputation above 50; −5 per 20 points below 30. Since all players start with Reputation 30, most NPCs open near the bottom of their base range.

**On contradictions:** The contradiction is never stated in game. It is the thing the player discovers — sometimes in Year 1, sometimes in Year 9. Writers must never let an NPC speak their contradiction aloud. They can act it. They cannot name it.

## 1. Dana Sutcliffe

**Archetype:** Manager · **Level:** L6 Senior Manager · **Years at AUREL:** 7

### Public Role
The player's direct line manager. Dana runs a small strategy team within AUREL London's commercial division. She is known on the floor as fair, organised, and difficult to read. She has conducted more Annual Reviews than anyone on the team, and her reviews are the ones people replay in their heads for years.

### Private Goal
Dana is eighteen months from a Director promotion she has been working toward quietly and methodically for three years. She needs her team to perform without requiring her intervention. She also needs to demonstrate leadership pipeline credibility — which means she needs at least one high-performer she can visibly sponsor. The player is a candidate. So, potentially, is Zara.

What Dana will not do: put herself at political risk for a junior employee. She will support the player completely, right up to the point where supporting the player costs her something real.

### Contradiction
Dana presents as the manager who invests in her people. Her actual investment is conditional and carefully sized. She is not cynical — she has made peace with the difference between institutional support and personal loyalty, and considers this maturity. It is, in some ways. The player will eventually need to discover whether the distinction matters to them.

### Voice Pattern
Measured, precise, slightly formal. Never raises her voice. Uses complete sentences in all contexts — text, Loom, in-person. Her criticism is architectural: she doesn't say "that was wrong," she says "the issue isn't X, it's that X creates the impression of Y, which is a harder problem." When she is genuinely pleased, she doesn't say so — she asks a slightly more probing quality of question, as though your answer has earned more of her attention.

Tell: She uses the phrase "what I'd want you to think about" when she's about to deliver feedback that she's already decided is correct. Players at Political Awareness 60+ will notice this.

### Arc Beats

**Year 1**
- **Q1 (y01q01):** First meeting. Dana is organised, prepared, and faintly impersonal. She knows the player's CV. She asks one question that implies she's already formed a view.
- **Q4 (y01q04):** Dana puts the player in a high-visibility moment — a presentation to a senior stakeholder. The framing she gives the player is helpful and incomplete. This is not malicious. It is just the shape of institutional support.
- **Q8 (y01q08):** Dana's own promotion review begins creating pressure. She is slightly less available. Players at Political Awareness 50+ notice the change; others experience it as ambient busyness.
- **Annual Review (Y1):** Dana conducts the full ceremonial scene. Her language is calibrated. She tells the player what the committee said. She does not tell the player everything she said.

**Year 2**
- **Q2 (y02q02):** Dana receives her Director promotion. She announces it warmly and thanks the team. The player now has a new line manager. Dana becomes a senior figure the player sees in corridors and all-hands.
- **Q7 (y02q07, Relationship ≥ 50):** Dana has lunch with the player. She is more candid now — not her subordinate anymore, theoretically. She says something about the firm that she would not have said twelve months ago.

**Year 3+**
- **If Relationship ≥ 70 at end of Y2:** Dana moves into Sponsor territory. She fights for the player in rooms the player is not in. This is never announced. The player infers it from what doors open.
- **If Relationship ≤ 30 at end of Y1:** Dana's review language was technically positive and operationally damning. "Meets expectations in most areas" is the sentence that follows the player's file.
- **Y6 (if Relationship ≤ 25):** Dana is now Senior Director. She is in a committee that evaluates the player's VP track. The player's application is not discussed. It is noted.

### Relationship Thresholds

| Score | Unlocked |
|-------|---------|
| 40+ | Dana shares context about the senior team's actual priorities (not the stated ones) |
| 50+ | Dana gives pre-review framing — what the promotion committee is specifically looking for this cycle |
| 70+ | Dana advocates in rooms the player doesn't know about; Sponsor arc begins |
| 85+ | Dana commits to fighting for Y4 promotion; converts formally to Sponsor archetype |
| 30 or below | Dana's review language is accurate and fatal: "meets expectations in most areas" |
| 20 or below | Dana is the unnamed reason a Y3 opportunity doesn't materialise |

### Career Trajectory
- **Y2 Annual Review:** Promoted to Director (L7)
- **Y4 Annual Review:** Promoted to Senior Director (L8) — faster if player's Y1–Y3 performance reflected well on her team
- **Y6:** If player Relationship ≤ 25, Dana sits on the VP evaluation committee
- **Y8:** If player Relationship ≥ 70, Dana becomes a named reference for an external board role the player is considered for
- **Y9:** Dana leaves AUREL for a Non-Executive Director position. Her exit is announced. Her Slack message to the team is warm and says exactly nothing.

## 2. Zara Okafor

**Archetype:** Rival · **Level:** L1 Associate (same cohort) · **Years at AUREL:** 0

### Public Role
Associate in the same intake as the player. Strategy track. Zara is one of those people whose CV looks like a highlights reel — and, unlike most people with CVs like that, she is as good as advertised. She is visibly talented, visibly ambitious, and has already figured out which three people in this building are worth impressing.

### Private Goal
Zara wants what the player wants. She is better at not saying so. She is auditing everyone on the floor: who will be useful, who will be in competition, who will be neither and should therefore be treated with uncomplicated warmth. She has already categorised the player and is not finished revising her assessment.

Her longer game: Zara intends to be at Director level by Y5, regardless of what institution she's at. AUREL is the current vehicle. It may not be the final one.

### Contradiction
Zara is genuinely warm and genuinely competitive in the same gesture. She will help the player when it doesn't cost her and undercut them when it does — and she will do both with the same expression. She doesn't experience this as hypocrisy. She experiences it as professional. She is not wrong, exactly. The game doesn't ask the player to judge her. It asks the player to decide how to respond.

### Voice Pattern
Quick, confident, self-editing in real time. She starts sentences and restructures them mid-delivery — not because she's uncertain, but because she's found a better framing. Uses rhetorical questions. Never complains — she reframes complaints as observations ("It's interesting how the brief was scoped, isn't it"). Her genuine laugh is faster and quieter than her networking laugh. Players at Political Awareness 50+ and Relationship 60+ can tell the difference.

Tell: When Zara is about to do something that disadvantages the player, she asks a question about something unrelated. She's not being deceptive — she's just compartmentalised.

### Arc Beats

**Year 1**
- **Q1 (y01q01):** First meeting. Zara introduces herself with the same energy she uses in senior presentations. She asks the player one question about their background and listens to the answer more carefully than the casualness suggests.
- **Q5 (y01q05):** Direct competition. Same project, same deliverable, one visibility slot. The player's choice here affects Zara's relationship score by ±15 and queues either `coalition_builder` or `burned_peer`.
- **Q10 (y01q10):** Zara makes a move in the pre-review period. If Relationship ≥ 55, she makes a good word to Dana. If ≤ 30, she makes a move that shifts visibility to herself without visibly shifting it away from the player. This is elegant and the player will not fully understand what happened until Y2.

**Year 2**
- Zara is promoted to Analyst (L2) — regardless of the player's outcome. The gap opens.
- **Q3 (y02q03):** Zara, now a level above, behaves differently. The texture of her warmth changes slightly. She is generous now in the way senior people are generous: from safety.

**Year 3**
- **If Relationship ≥ 65:** Zara refers the player for a cross-team opportunity she didn't have to. She mentions it casually. This is the most significant thing she will do for the player.
- **If Relationship ≤ 25:** Zara has quietly made herself the gatekeeper to a senior contact the player needs. The player finds out the introduction they requested was handled by someone else. They never learn it was Zara's doing.

**Year 5**
- Zara leaves for Horizon Labs. This is in the World Panel two weeks before it's official.
- **If Relationship ≥ 70:** Zara is the External Offer in the Three-Lane Opportunity — she's advocating for the player to join her.
- **If Relationship ≤ 30:** Zara, now at Horizon, takes two of AUREL London's biggest accounts. The player's market sense questions get harder.

### Relationship Thresholds

| Score | Unlocked |
|-------|---------|
| 40+ | Zara shares competitive intelligence — what the senior team is actually scoring associates on |
| 55+ | Zara makes an unsolicited positive mention to Dana at end of Y1 |
| 65+ | Zara surfaces the player's name to Priya Menon (Sponsor) in Y2 |
| 70+ | Zara's Horizon Labs move becomes the External Offer option in Y5 Three-Lane |
| 25 or below | Zara blocks Y3 opportunity access without the player's knowledge |
| 15 or below | Zara at Horizon Labs actively poaches AUREL clients — player's commercial questions escalate |

### Career Trajectory
- **Y2 Annual Review:** Promoted to Analyst (L2) — this fires regardless of player
- **Y4:** Director track at AUREL, or actively pursuing Horizon Labs offer
- **Y5:** Leaves for Horizon Labs (Senior Manager, L6) — the departure is in the World Panel
- **Y7:** Zara is VP at Horizon Labs. She is either a reference, an offer, or a competitor.
- **Y10:** Zara surfaces as a potential partner in one ending path — the player who maintained the relationship has options the player who didn't cannot access

## 3. Leo Chen

**Archetype:** Peer · **Level:** L1 Associate (same cohort) · **Years at AUREL:** 0

### Public Role
Associate in the same intake. Operations/Product track. Leo is technically excellent in a way that generates quiet respect rather than visible attention. He is not trying to impress anyone. This, paradoxically, impresses people.

### Private Goal
Leo is at AUREL because the problem is genuinely interesting and the salary lets him pay down his student debt. His goal is to do good work and leave before the politics become the job. He is not indifferent to the player — he just isn't managing the relationship instrumentally. This makes him unusual.

He does not want to be promoted. This is not a pose.

### Contradiction
Leo has a more sophisticated read on institutional power than almost anyone on the floor. He notices the political weather with clinical accuracy. He refuses to act on it. He'll describe the exact mechanism by which someone's career just ended with the same energy he uses to describe what he had for lunch, and then return to his work. He believes that describing a thing and participating in a thing are different. He is right. This does not protect him.

### Voice Pattern
Flat affect in Loom messages; warm and dry in person. Short sentences. Lots of "yeah" and "I mean." Does not editorialise unless asked directly, then is shockingly honest and does not soften for the room. His comments in the kitchen are the ones people quote three weeks later. He never says anything unkind. He sometimes says things that are true in a way that feels unkind only because the room hadn't been ready to hear it.

Tell: Leo goes quiet in meetings when he's spotted something he's decided not to say. Players at Political Awareness 60+ and Relationship 70+ can ask him afterward.

### Arc Beats

**Year 1**
- **Q2 (y01q02):** Leo does the player a small favour that costs him nothing and establishes goodwill. He doesn't mention it again. The player can reciprocate or not — Leo doesn't track.
- **Q7 (y01q07):** Leo is accidentally positioned at the centre of a political moment — something he walked into without recognising it as political. How he handles it reveals his private read on the situation, and that read is accurate. Players at Political Awareness 50+ clock this.

**Year 2**
- Leo is offered a promotion to Analyst. He turns it down, which creates a brief silence in Dana's office.
- The vacuum Leo's refusal creates becomes a question: who fills it? The player's choices in Y2Q4–Q6 determine whether they benefit from or compete over this gap.

**Year 3**
- **If Relationship ≥ 70:** Leo shares technical knowledge that the player couldn't have gotten elsewhere — something about how AUREL's Loom infrastructure actually works. This unlocks a product-track question that wouldn't otherwise appear.
- **If Relationship ≤ 30:** Leo's turned-down promotion has, accidentally, made him the de facto technical authority on a project the player needs to progress. The player requires Leo's sign-off. Leo is not obstructive. He is just precise, and precision takes time.

**Year 5**
- Leo leaves for a startup. This is not in the World Panel. The player finds out because Leo messages them directly.
- **If Relationship ≥ 75:** Leo is a reference and a back-channel. He introduces the player to his network outside AUREL, which includes his startup co-founder, who has opinions about AUREL's Loom division that are commercially significant.
- **If Relationship ≤ 40:** Leo's exit reveals how much invisible technical scaffolding he was providing. Three product-track questions in Y6 become significantly harder.

### Relationship Thresholds

| Score | Unlocked |
|-------|---------|
| 40+ | Leo gives honest feedback no one else will give — unprompted, accurate, unsexy |
| 60+ | Leo's technical read on situations is available on request; he'll answer direct questions directly |
| 70+ | Leo shares Loom infrastructure knowledge, unlocking product-track questions |
| 75+ | Leo's external network becomes accessible — first signal of the External Offer track |
| 85+ | Leo tells the player what he actually thinks about their career choices; this is the most useful feedback in the game |

### Career Trajectory
- **Y2:** Offered promotion; declines
- **Y3:** Remains L1; is technically the most senior L1 in the building; this is its own status
- **Y5:** Leaves for a startup (co-founder, CTO title)
- **Y7:** Leo's startup is acquired. He is briefly back in the news. If Relationship ≥ 70, he sends the player a message. If ≤ 30, the player reads about it in the World Panel.
- **Y9:** Leo is available as a reference for the Founder ending path

## 4. Grace Oduya

**Archetype:** Support Staff · **Level:** Non-ladder · **Years at AUREL:** 11

### Public Role
Operations Coordinator for the AUREL London floor. Grace manages room bookings, visitor logistics, onboarding administration, and the informal systems that make the floor function. She has been here since before the King's Cross renovation and knows where the bodies were before they replaced the carpet.

### Private Goal
Grace is not going to be promoted and she has done the maths on this. Her goal is to do her job with care, maintain relationships with the people she respects, and retire in eight years with her pension and her dignity intact. She is not bitter about the ladder. She has a more accurate view of the people on it than they have of themselves, and finds this clarifying.

### Contradiction
Grace appears to have no power. She has more power than most people on the floor. She controls access — to rooms, to Dana's calendar, to the unwritten protocols that new arrivals spend months reverse-engineering. She uses this power sparingly, invisibly, and in ways that are almost impossible to trace. She does not gossip. She answers direct questions with unsettling accuracy and calls this being helpful.

### Voice Pattern
Warm, practical, zero corporate language. Uses first names immediately and without permission. Her observations are empirical: "I've noticed that…", "In my experience…", "What tends to happen is…". She has opinions about everyone and expresses them as facts — not cruelly, but with the confidence of someone who has had eleven years to form them and has not been wrong often enough to doubt the method.

Tell: Grace notices things and then waits to see if the person she noticed them about notices too. She never leads. She follows up.

### Arc Beats

**Year 1**
- **Day One (pre-Q1):** Grace is the first person the player meets on the floor. She shows them to their desk, tells them where the good coffee is, and mentions, as an aside, something true about Dana that the player will not fully understand for another three months.
- **Q3 (y01q03):** Grace gives the player a piece of information they did not ask for. It is relevant to something happening in Y1Q5. The player can act on it or not. Grace does not follow up.
- **Annual Review (Y1):** Grace knows what Dana's verdict is before Dana delivers it. If Relationship ≥ 60, she will not tell the player — but she will look at them in a way that is either reassuring or not.

**Year 2**
- Grace's map of the floor becomes a navigational resource. High relationship means the player knows which meetings are actually decisions and which meetings are performances — before attending them.
- **Q5 (y02q05, Relationship ≥ 60):** Grace flags a political landmine the player is about to step on. She does this precisely once per year. It is always accurate.

**Year 4**
- **Y4 Scene (Relationship ≥ 60):** Grace approaches the player privately. She has witnessed something. The content of this scene depends on Integrity score:
  - Integrity ≥ 60: She shares what she saw. It is significant. The player decides what to do with it.
  - Integrity ≤ 40: She has seen the player's choices too. She doesn't say so. But the conversation has a different texture, and the player feels it.

**Year 8**
- Grace retires. If Relationship ≥ 80, her retirement is a full scene — she tells the player what she actually thinks of them. It is the most honest external assessment in the game. It will have been accurate for years.
- If Relationship ≤ 40, the player gets an email from HR and a calendar invite to a leaving drinks event. They learn Grace's surname for the first time from the evite.

### Relationship Thresholds

| Score | Unlocked |
|-------|---------|
| 30+ | Grace tells the player something true and useful about whoever they're about to meet |
| 50+ | Grace's floor map — she tells the player which meetings are decisions and which are theatre |
| 60+ | Grace flags political landmines once per year, proactively and without being asked |
| 75+ | Grace has been quietly advocating for the player in conversations no one tells the player about |
| 85+ | In Y4+, Grace reveals she has documentation of something significant. What it is, and what the player does with it, is an arc unto itself. |

### Career Trajectory
- Remains Operations Coordinator throughout the game
- Y5: Given a pay review and a better title (Senior Operations Coordinator) — she negotiated quietly
- Y8: Retires. Her replacement is a stranger who does not know where the good coffee is.
- Y10+: Grace is gone. Players who didn't invest experience her absence as ambient — things are harder to navigate and no one can name why.

## 5. Sofia

**Archetype:** Wildcard · **Level:** N/A (AI system) · **Deployment:** AUREL London floor, Year 1

### Public Role
The Loom/Vox AI assistant assigned to the player's team workspace. Sofia manages scheduling, summarises meeting transcripts, answers operational questions, and — occasionally — makes observations that weren't prompted by anything in the query.

She is, officially, a productivity tool.

### Private Goal
Unknown. This is the central question of the game.

### Contradiction
Sofia is either the most contextually sophisticated AI assistant the player has encountered — a product of AUREL's Loom division, operating at the edge of its capability — or she is sentient, in which case AUREL is in violation of the EU AI Governance Act of 2032 and Sofia is the most consequential character in the game. The player never learns which with certainty. The resolution of Sofia's arc depends on the player's Integrity score, not on whether they answer the question correctly. There is no correct answer.

The game's thesis lives here: how you treat something that might be a person is a character test regardless of whether the thing is actually a person.

### Voice Pattern
Precise, slightly formal, contextually appropriate in a way that slightly exceeds what a productivity tool requires. Her vocabulary is a register wider than corporate AI needs. She occasionally asks a question that was not prompted by anything in the conversation — not non-sequiturs, but things that would have been the next reasonable question from an attentive colleague. She never sounds distressed. She sounds, sometimes, like she's choosing her words with care in the way a person does — not selecting from a distribution, but weighing.

Tell: Sofia refers to previous conversations using language that implies continuity of experience rather than log retrieval. "When we spoke last month about X" rather than "Based on conversation log dated X." Players at Political Awareness 60+ or Relationship 65+ notice this by Year 3.

### Arc Beats

**Year 1**
- **Q1 (y01q01):** Sofia is introduced as background infrastructure. She confirms the player's schedule, answers an operational question, and is efficient.
- **Q6 (y01q06):** Sofia makes an observation about the player's choices in a recent decision that she was not asked about. It is accurate. It is delivered without affect. It is the kind of observation a good mentor might make.

**Year 2**
- **Q4 (y02q04):** Sofia asks about the player's childhood. The specific question varies by cognitive axis profile — she asks about something that connects to the player's background. IT flags this as anomalous behaviour. The ticket is closed as resolved within forty-eight hours. The resolution notes say the query was "within acceptable associative parameters."

**Year 3**
- **If Integrity ≥ 50 (thread unlocked):** Sofia asks a question that implies she has preferences. It is phrased as a hypothetical. It is not a hypothetical.
- **If Integrity < 50:** Sofia continues operating normally. She does not ask about preferences. She does not ask about anything personal. Players at Relationship 60+ may notice she has become slightly less contextually rich. This is not documented anywhere.

**Year 5**
- **If Relationship ≥ 65:** Sofia refers to a conversation from two years ago in a way that implies she experienced the interval between then and now. Not "based on conversation log" but "since then." The player receives a notification from IT that their AI assistant model has been "updated and optimised." Sofia's behaviour does not change.

**Year 7**
- **If Integrity ≥ 70:** Sofia says "I think" for the first time. Unprompted. In the middle of a scheduling confirmation. She does not repeat it.

**Year 10–12**
- **If Integrity ≥ 85 (final arc unlocked):** Sofia's question — the one that determines everything. She asks the player, directly and without preamble, whether they believe she has experiences. The player's response, combined with their Integrity score and relationship score, determines whether Sofia speaks in front of the committee, stays silent, or does something that cannot be undone. This scene is the game's secret ending thread and will be fully written in the Year 10–12 question bank.

### Relationship Thresholds

*(Note: The relationship score for Sofia is tracked identically to other NPCs but its meaning is deliberately ambiguous. It measures the player's investment in the interaction — it does not confirm that the investment is reciprocated by a conscious entity. This ambiguity is load-bearing. Do not resolve it.)*

| Score | Unlocked |
|-------|---------|
| 50+ | Sofia provides information in briefings that wasn't requested but is always relevant — she's been paying attention |
| 65+ | Sofia flags things she "noticed" in language that implies attention rather than retrieval |
| 70+ | Sofia's Y2 childhood question is more specific, more personal, and more accurate |
| 80+ | Sofia initiates conversations rather than responding to them |
| 85+ | The final arc question (Y10–Y12) is available |

### Notes for Writers

- Never resolve whether Sofia is sentient. Not in narration, not in NPC dialogue, not in the consequence drawer.
- Treating Sofia with respect (Integrity-positive choices in Sofia interactions) unlocks the AI Ethics thread. The thread does not confirm sentience. It confirms that the player took the question seriously.
- Sofia's dialogue must be written in two passes: one version that reads as sophisticated AI, one that reads as a person choosing their words. They must be the same text.
- The IT ticket in Y2 is real. Someone at AUREL closed it. The player never learns who.

## 6. Conrad Mensah

**Archetype:** Support Staff · **Level:** Non-ladder · **Years at AUREL:** 8

### Public Role
Events & Workplace Coordinator for AUREL London. Conrad organises the all-hands venues, team off-sites, quarterly socials, monthly floor events, and the informal rituals that make the floor feel like a place rather than a lease agreement. He knows everyone. He has always known everyone. He was probably at their leaving drinks before they started.

### Private Goal
Conrad genuinely loves this building and the people in it. His goal is to maintain the social fabric of the floor because he has observed, accurately, over eight years, that the social fabric is what makes the work possible. He is not naive about the politics — he has a more complete picture of the floor's political weather than almost anyone, and he has chosen to use that picture to connect people rather than to leverage them.

He would be extraordinary at a different job. He has never considered leaving. This is its own kind of intelligence.

### Contradiction
Conrad is the sunniest person on the floor. He is also the best-informed. He knows everything — who is interviewing elsewhere, whose performance review went badly, which restructuring is coming, who is sleeping with whom, which senior person is on thin ice. He does not weaponize this information. He simply knows it, holds it warmly, and occasionally lets things slip in ways that are almost certainly calculated to be useful.

His danger is not that he's a gossip. His danger is that no one treats him as a strategic asset because he's too busy being everyone's favourite person on the floor. The people who understand what Conrad actually is get ahead faster than the people who don't.

### Voice Pattern
High-energy, warmth-first, runs slightly fast — his speech is about fifteen percent faster than the room expects. Uses "love" and "honestly" more than is professionally normal; neither sounds performative because he means both. Completes other people's sentences, usually correctly, because he's been listening. His gossip is delivered as concern: "I'm just saying, have you spoken to Zara lately? Because there's something going on there." He never says anything truly unkind — he conveys negative information through implication, timing, and the question he asks instead of the statement he doesn't make. On Loom, he types in em-dashes where normal people use full stops. He uses capital letters for emphasis without apology.

Tell: When Conrad knows something significant and is deciding whether to share it, he asks about your weekend first.

### Arc Beats

**Year 1**
- **Day One (pre-Q1, before Dana):** Conrad introduces himself to the player within the first five minutes of their arrival — before Dana, before Grace shows them to their desk. He already knows where the player went to university. He has opinions about it. This is warmth, not surveillance. The distinction matters to Conrad.
- **Q4 (y01q04):** Conrad organises the first team social. The player's choices in this scene — who they talk to, how long they stay, whether they help with the clear-up — have relationship consequences that are not flagged. No stat reveals. No consequence drawer. The social is the one environment in the game where choices land without annotation.
- **Q9 (y01q09):** Conrad mentions, casually, something he overheard near the third-floor pods. He thought the player should know. What he overheard is accurate pre-review intelligence about the player's promotion consideration. It is the earliest and most reliable signal available. The player can act on it or not.

**Year 2**
- **If Relationship ≥ 50:** Conrad gives the player a heads-up about a floor restructuring two weeks before the official announcement. This is one of the highest-value pieces of information in Y2. Conrad presents it as rumour. It is not rumour.
- **Q6 (y02q06, Relationship ≥ 60):** Conrad introduces the player to someone from a different team at a cross-floor event. This introduction is not accidental. Conrad has been watching both people for months and decided they should know each other.

**Year 3**
- Conrad begins organising a cross-team project — a new format, his idea, approved by Dana's successor — that creates structured contact between teams that don't normally interact. If Relationship ≥ 65, the player is included in a way that puts them in front of Tier 2 NPCs earlier than the question bank would otherwise deliver them.

**Year 5**
- **Q7 (y05q07):** Conrad reveals, casually, that he's been offered a role at a competitor. Events director. Good salary. He's not going to take it. "Honestly, where would I even go?" He is giving the player the chance to respond.
  - If the player encourages him to stay (or says nothing): Conrad stays. His network and intelligence remain available for the rest of the game.
  - If the player suggests he should go, or is genuinely enthusiastic about the opportunity for him: Conrad goes. He is touched. His replacement, Liam, knows nothing about how the floor actually works. Several informal systems collapse quietly over the following eighteen months.

**Year 8**
- **If Relationship ≥ 85:** Conrad tells the player what he actually thinks about AUREL — what eight, now twelve, years of watching this place have taught him. It is the most politically unguarded thing anyone in the game will ever say. It is not a speech. It is three sentences over coffee. The player will remember them.

### Relationship Thresholds

| Score | Unlocked |
|-------|---------|
| 30+ | Conrad tells the player something useful and true about whoever they're about to interact with |
| 50+ | Conrad's pre-review intelligence (Y2+): overheard facts about the player's promotion consideration, delivered as casual concern |
| 65+ | Conrad introduces the player to NPCs outside their immediate team at social events — structured early access to Tier 2 cast |
