## 1. The Verdict (TL;DR)

We had six proposals. Three are usable as foundation, two are scaffolding, one is a stylish rough draft. We are not picking a winner. We are taking the best load-bearing decision from each and shipping a single coherent foundation.

**The bold calls:**

1. **Rename NXGEN → AUREL.** "NXGEN" reads like a placeholder a startup accelerator dies in. AUREL reads like a company whose investor relations team can weaponize serif fonts. (GPT, GPT-thinking)
2. **HQ: Chicago, not Austin.** Austin is the consensus answer; Chicago is the better one — it gives us scale, architectural seriousness, and distance from coastal cliché without sacrificing the "not Silicon Valley" requirement. (GPT, GPT-thinking)
3. **Player starts in London, King's Cross.** Restored Art Deco / rail-side warehouse aesthetic. Officially a satellite, in practice a power node. (Long ASCEND + GPT)
4. **Three flagship products: Halo, Loom, Glassroom.** Wearable + AI orchestration layer + spatial collaboration. Halo is the iPhone-equivalent; Loom is the OS-of-your-life; Glassroom is the conference-room successor. (GPT, with Long ASCEND's Vox flavor folded into Loom.)
5. **12-year arc, 12-level ladder.** Match arc to ladder so promotion year-by-year makes structural sense. (GPT-thinking)
6. **10 visible attributes + 4 hidden long-arc pillars.** The pillars are Egon Zehnder (Curiosity, Insight, Engagement, Determination). The visible ten are corporate-language stats. **Add Integrity as a visible stat** — every proposal under-weighted it, and the game's ethical arc dies without it. (synthesis)
7. **25 NPCs total. 5 active in MVP Year 1.** Use Long ASCEND's specific cast as the spine — Priya Menon, Zara Okafor, Dana Sutcliffe, Dr. Ruth Abara, Leo Chen, Anya Petrov, Sofia (the maybe-sentient Vox AI), Grace Oduya. Augment with GPT's Mara Velez and Naomi Cade in later years.
8. **Annual Review is a human scene, not an HR avatar.** Reject Nemotron's robotic HR avatar — it's cold, it kills the wit, and it makes the year's most important moment feel like a CAPTCHA. The manager looks you in the face and says the number aloud.
9. **Tech: HTML5 + TypeScript + Vite + JSON content + custom Ink-inspired narrative engine + GitHub Pages.** Universal across proposals; not in dispute.
10. **Visual identity: typography-first, dark-mode default, Inter + Source Serif 4 + JetBrains Mono.** Slate `#0A0A0F`, Pearl `#F4F1EA`, Oxide `#C75B39`, Gold `#D4AF37`. Reference: high-end annual report meets Wes Anderson set.
11. **No gamification confetti. No XP bars. No streaks. No loot boxes.** The game's monetization must hold up to its own values: premium one-time purchase + expansion chapters + cosmetic UI themes. That's it.
12. **Onboarding: 5-step wizard.** Name → Background → Traits → Work-style preview → "First-day Letter." (GPT-thinking — best framing.)

If a future contributor wants to overturn any of these, they need a written argument better than the one being overturned.

## 3. The merged design (one pass)

### 3.1 Premise

You are a fresh graduate joining **AUREL London** in 2034. AUREL is the consumer-tech behemoth that ate identity, payments, presence, and collaboration. You will spend twelve in-game years navigating ten major decisions per year. There are no right answers. Every choice is a self-portrait.

### 3.2 Tone

Dry, sharp, observational. Funny because it is true. Cynical about systems, not nihilistic about people. Every joke must also be a diagnosis — if a line gets a laugh but tells the player nothing about status, incentives, or human behavior, cut it.

**The Galloway/Godin Test:** Would Galloway find this funny? Would Godin believe it? If either answer is no, rewrite.

### 3.3 The world of 2034 (one paragraph, not one chapter)

AI didn't end work; it compressed junior work and made judgment, taste, and trust scarce. Email is demoted. Meetings are 15-minute syncs around AI pre-reads or 90-minute deep sessions with no screens. London is Europe's regulatory translator and a satellite-that-isn't. Everyone performs wellness while quietly terrified of obsolescence. Sponsorship — a senior person fighting for you behind closed doors — replaced manager goodwill as the only reliable promotion mechanism. Ambition is fashionable again, but must be worn lightly.

### 3.4 The company: AUREL

- **Founded:** 2026 by **Elena Voss** (ex-McKinsey, then 34). Pivoted from a struggling enterprise identity firm into consumer hardware after a 2028 acquisition.
- **HQ:** Chicago. Reclaimed warehouse-district campus.
- **Player office:** AUREL London, King's Cross. Restored Art Deco rail-side building. Looks like a gallery with compliance obligations.
- **Three product pillars:**
  - **Halo** — premium ambient wearable; identity, payments, AI assistance, presence.
  - **Loom** — life middleware OS; brokers your communications, scheduling, identity across services.
  - **Glassroom** — spatial collaboration hardware + software for boardrooms, universities, and high-end homes.
- **Stated values vs unwritten rules** — see Company Bible (separate doc, owned by Narrative Lead). Keep the gap wide. That gap is the game.
- **Ladder (12 levels):** L1 Associate → L2 Analyst → L3 Senior Analyst → L4 Lead → L5 Manager → L6 Senior Manager → L7 Director → L8 Senior Director → L9 VP → L10 SVP → L11 EVP → L12 C-Suite.

### 3.5 Player creation

Five-step onboarding wizard:

1. **Name** + pronouns (free text).
2. **Background** — 1 of 7. PPE at LSE; CS Dropout; Engineering, State Uni; Philosophy + MBA; Art History, Somehow; International Student (Visa Clock); Apprenticeship / Portfolio Kid. Each grants 2 starting attribute bonuses and flavors early dialogue.
3. **Traits** — pick 3 from 12. Behavioral language only ("You read the room before you enter it"). MBTI mapping is hidden — exposed only as which dialogue lines appear.
4. **Work-style preview** — narrative paragraph reflecting the choices ("You'll prepare more than the room expected and resent the room for not noticing").
5. **First-day letter** — a one-page in-fiction welcome that sets up the opening scene.

### 3.6 Attribute system

**10 visible stats (0–100):**
| Stat | What it does |
|---|---|
| Political Awareness | Reveals subtext; unlocks "read between the lines" options |
| Strategic Influence | How much your opinion weighs at senior tables |
| Collaborative Pull | Peer trust; whether teams volunteer for your projects |
| Resilience | Recovery from setbacks; mitigates negative consequence severity |
| Execution Discipline | Delivering reliably under pressure; gates performance score |
| Adaptive Nerve | Comfort with risk; unlocks bold plays and external offers |
| Technical Credibility | Whether engineers respect you; gates product/CTO track |
| Reputation | Ambient prestige inside the firm; affects indirect NPC reactions |
| Market Sense | Customer/commercial judgment; unlocks senior commercial paths |
| **Integrity** | Alignment between action and values; gates ethical arcs and certain endings |

**4 hidden long-arc pillars (Egon Zehnder; grow 1–3 pts/year, gate L9+):**
Curiosity, Insight, Engagement, Determination.

**4 hidden cognitive axes (MBTI-derived; affect dialogue phrasing, opportunity detection, NPC reactions):**
Social Energy, Signal Reading, Decision Lens, Operating Style.

We do not show the player MBTI labels. Ever. We show them themselves.

### 3.7 Core mechanic — 10 questions per year

**Year shape:**
- Q1–Q2: orientation
- Q3–Q4: first tests
- Q5–Q7: relationships form, first consequences
- Q8–Q9: pre-review pressure / political compression
- Q10: climax decision that colors the Annual Review

**Question structure:**
- Setup text: 100–200 words, narrative non-fiction voice
- 4 options: 20–40 words each
- Each option: 2–4 attribute deltas (revealed *after* selection, in a results drawer with one line of internal monologue)
- Each option: pushes 0–3 hidden tags into the **consequence queue** (e.g., `seen_by_sponsor`, `burned_peer`, `integrity_debt_minor`, `latent_offer`)
- Locking: questions can be gated by stats, NPC relationship thresholds, or earlier choices

**Iron rules:**
- No saint option. No idiot option. No joke option that isn't also true.
- Every option offers one gain and one cost.
- The save fires after every choice. There is no undo. Live with it.

### 3.8 Annual Review (the ritual)

Three acts in a single ceremonial scene.

**(a) Compensation.** A scene, not a spreadsheet. Manager says the number aloud. Formula:
- 40% attribute aggregate (Execution Discipline, Reputation, Credibility)
- 30% performance from year's choices
- 20% sponsor strength
- 10% hidden Political Capital

**(b) Promotion.** Three outcomes — Promoted / Not Promoted (with reason) / **Promoted Conditionally** (title without authority, "acting" role, prove-it year). Conditional is the most interesting outcome and should fire often.

**Promotion Scorecard** — surfaced from Q8 onward as four bands per dimension (Delivery / Visibility / Trust / Readiness): *Below line / In discussion / Likely / Sponsor-dependent*. Tension, not surprise.

**(c) Three-Lane Opportunity:**
- **Internal Mobility** — new team, project, geography
- **External Offer** — competitor approach (e.g., Horizon Labs)
- **Strategic Relationship** — sponsor escalates, mentor commits, rival makes a move

Every lane shows visible trade-offs and hides 1–2 consequences that emerge 1–2 years later.

### 3.9 NPC system

**25 named characters across 9 archetypes:**
- 2 Sponsors · 3 Rivals · 4 Managers · 2 Mentors · 4 Peers · 3 Direct Reports (unlock Y4+) · 3 Clients · 2 Wildcards · 2 Support Staff

**Per-NPC contract:** public role · private goal · contradiction · voice pattern · arc beats by year · independent career trajectory (they get promoted, leave, return, die).

**Relationship Score 0–100** with five thresholds:
- 0–24 Cold · 25–49 Functional · 50–69 Warm · 70–84 Trusted · 85–100 Invested.

**The Wildcard we are protecting:** **Sofia** — the Vox/Loom AI assigned to the player's team who occasionally has opinions. If sentient, AUREL is in breach of EU AI law. If not, why did she ask about the player's childhood? Treating Sofia with respect unlocks an entire AI Ethics thread. This NPC is the game's secret thesis.

### 3.10 UX — the cutting-edge spec

**Design principles:**
1. Typography is the medium. Words must feel expensive.
2. Dark mode default. Light mode is a concession.
3. Nothing shouts. Everything responds.
4. No gamification debris. No XP bars bouncing. Rewards are narrative.
5. Premium product feel — consistent with a company that makes beautiful objects.

**The 8 components:**

1. **Decision Screen** — full-bleed dark background. Centered narrative text, max-width 640px, line length 65–75ch. Four "conversation cards" below (not radio buttons). Hover lift `translateY(-2px)`. After choice, cards collapse and a slim **Consequences Drawer** opens showing stat deltas with directional arrows + one line of inner monologue.
2. **Attribute Panel** — right-side collapsible drawer (desktop) / modal (mobile). Stat values as text-led horizontal bars (numbers hidden until hover — "feel growth before you measure it"). Long-arc pillars grouped separately with distinct visual weight (rings, not bars).
3. **People Panel — Constellation Map.** Player at center. NPCs orbit in rings (inner = close, outer = distant). Line color: gold (positive) / grey (neutral) / crimson (negative) / dashed (suspected/hidden). Pulse = active storyline. Sortable list view as the accessibility alternative — non-negotiable.
4. **Career Timeline** — horizontal year ribbon. Each year a card: headline, comp number, promotion icon, 3–4 chosen decisions as story snippets. Hover surfaces post-hoc narrative ("You didn't know it then, but this was the year you chose loyalty over ambition.").
5. **Annual Review Screen** — letterboxed, distinct color temperature (warmer), slower motion. Manager text portrait. Compensation appears after a dramatic pause. Cinematic, not gamified.
6. **World Panel** — pull-down ambient feed. AI-summarized "narrative streams" (in-fiction nod to Loom). Headlines, AUREL announcements, 2034 cultural micro-trends. High Curiosity unlocks hidden questions from this feed.
7. **Journal** — searchable log of every question, choice, and a brief reflective note in the game's voice. Tagged by NPC, theme, consequence.
8. **Onboarding Flow** — 5-step wizard. Each step previews implications in prose, not numbers.

**Visual identity:**
- **Type:** Inter (UI/headings) · Source Serif 4 (narrative body) · JetBrains Mono (data, "system" text). Fallback: IBM Plex stack.
- **Palette:** Slate `#0A0A0F` · Pearl `#F4F1EA` · Oxide `#C75B39` · Graphite `#6B7280` · Gold `#D4AF37`. Light mode: parchment / slate / smoke / muted teal / burgundy.
- **Motion:** 160–220ms ease-in-out. Text reveals via opacity, never typewriter. Cards use subtle parallax on hover. Ceremonial moments only get micro-parallax. No bounce. No confetti. No emoji explosions.

**Cutting-edge usability commitments (added by CCO):**
- **Sortable list alternatives** for every spatial visualization. Constellation maps are beautiful; not everyone can use them.
- **Keyboard-first navigation.** Every choice tile, drawer, and panel reachable without mouse. ARIA roles correct from day one.
- **Reading-mode toggle.** A serif-only, single-column reading mode for players who want the prose without the chrome.
- **Narrative streams in the World Panel** — pulled live from a JSON feed; these are the in-fiction equivalent of Slack/RSS, and they make the world feel like it's running without the player.
- **Save-state diff view** — at any point the player can see "what changed in the last decision" — a quiet but powerful affordance no narrative RPG currently offers.
- **i18n hooks from day one.** Keys not sentences. RTL CSS. Text-expansion tested.
- **Anonymous, opt-in telemetry** — what to measure: completion rate, choice distribution, drop-off, post-year-end "what did you learn?" survey.

### 3.11 Tech stack — settled

| Layer | Tool | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Language | TypeScript | Type-safe content schemas, refactor-friendly |
| Bundler | Vite | Fast dev, no config swamp |
| Styling | PostCSS + small utility set (NOT Tailwind from day one) | Typography-first design needs hand-tuned CSS, not utility soup. Reconsider Tailwind for V1. |
| State | Custom immutable store, vanilla TS | Redux is overkill |
| Content | JSON + YAML | Writers ship without dev help |
| Narrative | Custom Ink-inspired engine | Ink's good ideas (divert, tags) without Ink's runtime |
| Persistence | localStorage + JSON export/import | Save files are JSON, period |
| Deployment | GitHub Pages + GitHub Actions | Free, versioned, CDN, content PRs reviewed like code |
| Rendering | DOM + CSS animations | No Canvas. No WebGL. Exception: People Panel constellation may use SVG/D3 force simulation if it gets dense. |

**Reject:** Twine (state-thin), Ink-as-runtime (too opinionated for our state-driven branching), React/Svelte (overkill for MVP — revisit at V1.0 if UI complexity demands it), Electron (web is the platform), no-code narrative tools (premium UX needs bespoke).

### 3.12 Monetization — line in the sand

- Base game (Years 1–3): free.
- The Full Ascent (Years 4–12): one-time purchase.
- Cosmetic UI themes ("1998 Internet" mode, "Paper Memo" mode): microtransactions.
- **No** loot boxes. **No** subscription. **No** energy timers. **No** dark patterns. **No** pay-to-promote nonsense.

If the game can't hold up to its own values, it has no business existing.
